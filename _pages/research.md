## Mechanistic Mathematical Modelling 

<li style="font-family: Georgia, serif; font-size: 16px;">
<p style="text-align: justify;">
Building a computational vertex model to investigate the mechanical principles governing epithelial bending 
during tissue invagination, focusing on how changes in cell shape, contractility, and junctional tension drive 
morphogenesis. Deriving the energy equations for the vertexes of a cell including contributions from area elasticity, perimeter elasticity, line tension, and active contractile forces.  
</p>
**PI:** [Dr Bandan Chakroborthy](https://www.bandanchakrabortty.com/)  
**Institute:** IISER Thiruvananthapuram
 
## Computational Modelling
<p style="text-align: justify;">
Working on agent-based modeling, where I studied the role of stromal cell dynamics in epithelial tissue folding in the mouse uterus using CompuCell3D. I developed a model to simulate epithelial folding in response to a morphogen and investigated how perturbations in stromal behavior influence epithelial topology.  
</p>
**PI:** [Dr Ripla Arora](https://www.aroralab.com/)  
**Institute:** Michigan State University

## Computational Metagenomics
<p style="text-align: justify;">
To investigate the relationship between cardiometabolic markers such as Glucose, Triglycerides, HDL, and LDL- and gut bacteria, I conducted a comprehensive metagenomic analysis using data curated from 20 independent studies. These studies, derived from various research papers, provided patient-specific metagenomic profiles, with the markers serving as target variables and the gut bacterial abundances as features. we developed a custom script to explore these associations. I implemented a supervised machine learning approach using Random
Forest models to evaluate each study data for every marker.  
</p>
**PI:** [Dr Tarini Shankar Ghosh](https://microbiome.iiitd.edu.in/)  
**Institute:** IIIT Delhi
